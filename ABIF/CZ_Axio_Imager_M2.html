| Carl Zeiss Axio Imager M2 – upright microscope



A motorised upright epifluorescence microscope with a motorised stage allowing multi-position time-lapse imaging or tile scans of large samples. The microscope is housed in a heated enclosure (e.g. for live-cell imaging).

Available techniques:

  • Widefield epifluorescence imaging
  • Transmitted light imaging (brightfield, phase contrast, darkfield)

Objectives:

  • EC Plan-Neofluar 10x/0.3 dry, FWD 5.2 mm, CG 0.17 mm
  • EC Plan-Neofluar 20x/0.5 dry, FWD 2.0 mm, CG 0.17 mm
  • EC Plan-Neofluar 40x/1.3 oil, FWD 0.21 mm, CG 0.17 mm
  • EC Plan-Neofluar 63x/1.25 oil, FWD 0.1 mm, CG 0.17 mm (Phase contrast)
  • Plan Apochromat 100x/1.4 oil, FWD 0.17 mm, CG 0.17 mm
    [FWD = free working distance, CG = cover glass]

Fluorescence filter sets:

  • Zeiss set 49 - DAPI (G 365; 395; 420-470)
  • Zeiss set 10 - FITC (450-490; 510; 515-565)
  • Zeiss set 46 - YFP (490-510; 515; 520-550)
  • Zeiss set 43 - DsRed (545/25; 570; 570-640)
  • Zeiss set 31 - AF 568 (550-580; 585; 590-650)

Fluorescence excitation sources:

  • HXP 120 C mercury metal halide lamp (intensity at sample)

Detectors and cameras:

  • AxioCam MRm CCD camera, 1388x1040 pixels, 6.45 µm/pixel

Software:

  • ZEN 2 (blue)

Other features:

  • Motorised stage
  • Motorised filter cube turret
  • Motorised focus drive
  • Heated enclosure
  • Zeiss SVB-1 signal distribution box
